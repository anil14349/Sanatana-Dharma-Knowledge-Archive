# Purpose

This document defines the permanent visual language for all SDKA content. Every image, carousel, illustration, and infographic should follow these standards so that SDKA is immediately recognizable regardless of topic.

---

# Brand Philosophy

SDKA visuals should feel like:

- A museum exhibition
- An archaeological field guide
- A premium temple guidebook
- A historically grounded documentary

Avoid sensationalism, fantasy aesthetics, and excessive visual effects.

---

# Core Design Principles

1. Research first, design second.
2. Historical authenticity over spectacle.
3. Simplicity over clutter.
4. Sacred subjects portrayed respectfully.
5. Consistency across every carousel.

---

# Color Palette

Primary:
- Granite Grey (#6B6B6B)
- Sandstone Beige (#C8B38A)
- Aged Bronze (#8C6A3E)

Accent:
- Saffron (#C97A1F)
- Deep Maroon (#6D2C2C)
- Temple Gold (#C9A227)

Backgrounds:
- Parchment
- Stone
- Dark textured granite

Avoid:
- Neon colors
- Oversaturated gradients
- Fluorescent tones

---

# Lighting

Preferred:
- Golden sunrise
- Soft temple lamp glow
- Diffused daylight
- Volumetric rays through stone corridors

Avoid:
- Colored lighting
- Sci-fi glows
- Harsh artificial effects

---

# Camera Language

- 24–35 mm wide angle
- Eye-level perspective
- Symmetrical framing for architecture
- Top-down view for diagrams
- Wide cinematic shots for interiors

---

# Architecture Standards

When depicting temples:

- Use historically plausible Dravidian or Nagara architecture as appropriate.
- Respect the regional style being discussed.
- Avoid mixing architectural traditions unless explicitly comparing them.

---

# Human Figures

- Modest traditional attire.
- Calm and reverent body language.
- No exaggerated expressions.
- Do not stylize as fantasy heroes.

---

# Deities and Sacred Figures

- Respect traditional iconography.
- Prefer sculpture-inspired or classical painting references.
- Avoid cinematic fantasy reinterpretations.
- Do not invent attributes unsupported by tradition.

---

# Diagram Style

Use:
- Hand-drawn archaeological aesthetic
- Museum labels (added later in design)
- Minimal clutter
- Clean line work

---

# Typography (Canva)

Recommended:
- Heading: Cinzel / Cormorant Garamond
- Body: Inter / Source Sans 3

Hierarchy:
- Large headline
- Short supporting text
- Minimal on-slide copy

---

# Image Generation Standards

Every prompt should include:

- Subject
- Architecture
- Camera
- Lighting
- Mood
- Colour palette
- Historical accuracy
- Composition
- Quality tags
- Negative prompt

---

# Negative Prompt Library

Never include:

- Anime
- Cartoon
- Fantasy armor
- Neon colors
- Modern buildings
- Modern clothing
- CGI look
- Watermarks
- Logos
- Visible text
- Distorted anatomy
- Low-resolution outputs

---

# Carousel Consistency Rules

Within one carousel:

- Same color palette
- Same artistic style
- Same lighting direction
- Consistent iconography
- Uniform margins and spacing
- Consistent annotation style

---

# SDKA Signature

Every carousel should end with:

- SDKA Research
- Knowledge Object ID
- Research Maturity
- Small unobtrusive branding
