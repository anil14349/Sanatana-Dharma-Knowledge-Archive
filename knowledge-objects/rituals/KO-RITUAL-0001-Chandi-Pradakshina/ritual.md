# Ritual
**Knowledge Object:** KO-RITUAL-0001 — Chandi Pradakshina

## Purpose

This document describes the ritual commonly known as **Chandi Pradakshina** using an evidence-based approach. It documents observed practice without prescribing ritual behaviour.

---

# Guiding Principle

SDKA distinguishes between:

- Agamic prescription
- Temple-specific practice
- Regional tradition
- Oral explanation
- Popular belief

These categories should never be treated as interchangeable.

---

# General Ritual Sequence

In many Shaiva temples that preserve this tradition, devotees typically:

1. Offer darshana of the Shiva Linga.
2. Begin clockwise circumambulation.
3. Respect the Soma Sutra (Pranala) according to the temple's tradition.
4. Reach the Chandikeshvara shrine.
5. Return by the prescribed path where applicable.
6. Conclude worship following the temple's customary practice.

This is a generalized description. Individual temples may differ.

---

# Role of the Soma Sutra

The Soma Sutra is the channel through which Abhisheka water exits the sanctum.

In many Agamic traditions it is treated with reverence because it carries water that has touched the Shiva Linga. This is the primary reason many temples advise devotees not to cross it.

Evidence Level: **A**

Confidence: **High**

---

# Role of Chandikeshvara

Chandikeshvara is revered as a guardian of Shiva's temple and offerings.

Many South Indian temples incorporate his shrine into the circumambulation route. Customs associated with his shrine, such as pausing or offering reverence, vary by temple.

Evidence Level: **A/B**

Confidence: **High**

---

# Regional Practice

Practices differ across:

- Tamil Nadu
- Andhra Pradesh
- Telangana
- Karnataka
- Kerala
- North India

Always follow the guidance provided by the temple authorities and priests.

---

# Practices Requiring Caution

The following statements should **not** be presented as universal facts:

- Every Shiva temple follows Chandi Pradakshina.
- Crossing the Soma Sutra is forbidden everywhere.
- Finger snapping before Chandikeshvara is mandatory.
- All temples share the same circumambulation path.

These claims require regional qualification.

---

# Devotional Significance

Chandi Pradakshina reminds devotees that temple architecture, sacred water, guardians, and worship form a unified ritual system. The path itself becomes an act of reverence rather than a simple walk around the sanctum.
