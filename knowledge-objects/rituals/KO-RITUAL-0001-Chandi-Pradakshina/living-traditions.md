# Living Traditions
**Knowledge Object:** KO-RITUAL-0001 — Chandi Pradakshina

## Purpose

This document records contemporary temple practices associated with Chandi Pradakshina. It distinguishes observed living traditions from Agamic prescriptions and highlights regional diversity.

---

# SDKA Classification

| Category | Description |
|----------|-------------|
| Agamic Prescription | Directly supported by primary ritual texts |
| Temple Practice | Customs followed in a specific temple |
| Regional Tradition | Practices common within a geographic region |
| Oral Tradition | Explanations transmitted by priests or devotees |
| Popular Belief | Claims requiring validation |

---

# Commonly Observed Practices

## Respect for the Soma Sutra

Many Shaiva temples instruct devotees to avoid crossing the Soma Sutra (Pranala) because it carries Abhisheka water from the sanctum.

**Evidence:** Agamic tradition, temple practice  
**Confidence:** High

---

## Reverence to Chandikeshvara

In many South Indian temples, devotees pause at the Chandikeshvara shrine as part of their circumambulation.

Observed practices include:

- Offering namaskara.
- Silent reverence.
- Temple-specific customs.

These practices vary and should not be generalized.

**Confidence:** High

---

## Temple Guidance

Temple authorities may prescribe different circumambulation routes depending on:

- Temple architecture.
- Local Agamic tradition.
- Restoration work.
- Festival arrangements.

Visitors should follow instructions displayed by the temple or given by priests.

# Regional Observations

## Tamil Nadu

Strong continuity of Agamic worship with clearly defined temple customs in many historic Shaiva temples.

## Andhra Pradesh & Telangana

Practices vary by temple, with local priestly guidance playing an important role.

## Karnataka

Architectural diversity influences ritual movement.

## Kerala

Distinct Tantric and architectural traditions require independent documentation.

## North India

Temple customs are diverse and should be evaluated individually.
