
# Temple Architecture
**Knowledge Object:** KO-RITUAL-0001 — Chandi Pradakshina

## Purpose

This document explains the architectural context required to understand Chandi Pradakshina. In Shaiva temples, ritual movement is guided by sacred architecture rather than arbitrary custom.

---

# Core Principle

In Agamic temple design, architecture and ritual are inseparable. The placement of each structural element influences how devotees approach, worship, and circumambulate the sanctum.

---

# Principal Components

## Garbhagriha
The innermost sanctum containing the Shiva Linga. It is the spiritual center of the temple.

## Antarala
The vestibule connecting the sanctum and the hall of worship.

## Mukhamandapa
The public worship hall where devotees gather before approaching the sanctum.

## Nandi
Aligned directly with the Shiva Linga, symbolizing devotion and the axis of worship.

## Soma Sutra (Pranala)
A stone outlet through which Abhisheka water leaves the sanctum. In many Shaiva traditions, devotees avoid crossing this outlet during circumambulation.

## Chandikeshvara Shrine
Usually located along the northern side of many South Indian Shaiva temples. In many traditions it serves as the turning point during Chandi Pradakshina.

## Bali Peetha
The altar for ritual offerings to attendant deities.

## Dhwaja Stambha
The ceremonial flagstaff marking the temple's ritual axis.

# Architectural Observations

- The Soma Sutra is both an architectural and ritual feature.
- The placement of Chandikeshvara may vary between temples.
- Chola temples exhibit highly integrated ritual planning.
- Regional architectural traditions influence circumambulation paths.

---

# Relationship to Chandi Pradakshina

The circumambulatory path is determined by the sacred arrangement of the temple rather than convenience. Understanding the layout helps explain why many Shaiva traditions prescribe avoiding the Soma Sutra and turning near Chandikeshvara.
