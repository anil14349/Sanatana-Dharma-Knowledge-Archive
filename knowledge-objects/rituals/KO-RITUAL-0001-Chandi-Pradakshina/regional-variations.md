# Regional Variations
**Knowledge Object:** KO-RITUAL-0001 — Chandi Pradakshina

## Purpose

This document records how the practice associated with Chandi Pradakshina varies across different regions of India. SDKA treats regional diversity as an essential part of documenting Sanatana Dharma accurately.

---

# Comparative Overview

| Region | Agamic Continuity | Chandi Pradakshina | Confidence |
|--------|-------------------|--------------------|------------|
| Tamil Nadu | Strong | Widely observed in many traditional Shaiva temples | High |
| Andhra Pradesh | Strong | Temple dependent | Medium |
| Telangana | Strong | Temple dependent | Medium |
| Karnataka | Moderate–Strong | Regional variation | Medium |
| Kerala | Distinct Tantric traditions | Independent documentation required | Medium |
| North India | Diverse traditions | Not universal | Medium |

---

# Tamil Nadu

Characteristics:

- Strong Shaiva Agamic continuity.
- Numerous Chola and post-Chola temples.
- Chandikeshvara shrine commonly present.
- Soma Sutra integrated into temple planning.

Representative temples:

- Chidambaram
- Brihadeeswarar
- Kapaleeshwarar
- Ekambareswarar

Confidence: High

---

# Andhra Pradesh

Representative temples:

- Srisailam
- Srikalahasti

Current evidence indicates that temple authorities guide devotees according to local Agamic practice.

Confidence: Medium

---

# Telangana

Large historic Shaiva temples continue Agamic worship, but circumambulation patterns should be documented individually.

Confidence: Medium

---

# Karnataka

Temple architecture reflects Chalukya, Hoysala, and Vijayanagara influences.

Regional differences should be documented before generalizing ritual practice.

Confidence: Medium

---

# Kerala

Kerala temples preserve distinct architectural and Tantric traditions.

Practices should not automatically be equated with Tamil Shaiva traditions.

Confidence: Medium

---

# North India

Temple layouts and ritual customs vary considerably.

Current evidence does not support presenting Chandi Pradakshina as a universal North Indian practice.

Confidence: Medium

---

# Research Gaps

Future SDKA work should include:

- Field verification at major temples.
- Temple signboards and visitor guidance.
- Priest interviews where appropriate.
- Comparative architectural mapping.
- Regional Agama comparison.

---

# Key Principle

Whenever regional evidence differs, SDKA documents the variation rather than forcing a single generalized conclusion.
