# Misconceptions
**Knowledge Object:** KO-RITUAL-0001 — Chandi Pradakshina

## Purpose

This document identifies common misconceptions surrounding Chandi Pradakshina and evaluates each claim using the SDKA evidence framework. The goal is to distinguish verified knowledge from oversimplified or unsupported assertions.

---

# SDKA Evaluation Scale

| Verdict | Meaning |
|---------|---------|
| ✅ Supported | Backed by primary or strong supporting evidence |
| 🟡 Partially Supported | True in some traditions or regions |
| ❌ Unsupported | No reliable evidence currently verified |
| 🔍 Under Research | Insufficient evidence; further validation required |

---

# Misconception Audit

## Claim 1

**"Every Shiva temple follows Chandi Pradakshina."**

**Verdict:** ❌ Unsupported

### Explanation

Temple architecture, Agamic lineage, and regional customs differ considerably. While many traditional South Indian Shaiva temples preserve this practice, it should not be generalized to every Shiva temple.

Evidence Level: D

---

## Claim 2

**"Crossing the Soma Sutra is forbidden everywhere."**

**Verdict:** 🟡 Partially Supported

### Explanation

Many Agamic traditions and temples advise devotees not to cross the Soma Sutra because it carries Abhisheka water. However, practice varies depending on the temple and local tradition.

Evidence Level: A/B

---

## Claim 3

**"Finger snapping before Chandikeshvara is mandatory."**

**Verdict:** ❌ Unsupported

### Explanation

Finger snapping is observed in some regional traditions but has not been verified as a universal Agamic requirement.

Evidence Level: C

---

## Claim 4

**"Chandikeshvara always stands in the same location."**

**Verdict:** 🟡 Partially Supported

### Explanation

The shrine commonly appears along the northern side in many South Indian temples, but architectural layouts vary.

Evidence Level: B

---

## Claim 5

**"One ritual rule applies to every Shaiva temple."**

**Verdict:** ❌ Unsupported

### Explanation

Shaiva worship is preserved through multiple Agamic schools, temple traditions, and regional lineages. SDKA documents these differences rather than forcing a single generalized rule.

Evidence Level: D

---

# Lessons for Researchers

When documenting temple practice:

- Verify claims with primary sources whenever possible.
- Separate Agamic prescriptions from living traditions.
- Record regional differences explicitly.
- Avoid universal statements unless supported by evidence.
