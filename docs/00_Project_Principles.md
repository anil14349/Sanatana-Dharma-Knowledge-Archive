# 00 – Project Principles

**Document Version:** 4.0  
**Project:** Sanatana Dharma Knowledge Archive (SDKA)  
**Status:** Foundational Constitution  
**Last Updated:** June 2026

---

## Guiding Principle

> **Research First → Understanding Second → Storytelling Third → Virality Last**

Every decision in this project — content, design, platform, tooling — is evaluated against this hierarchy. Virality is welcomed but never pursued at the cost of authenticity.

---

## Core Principles

### 1. Authenticity Above All

- Never distort traditional teachings for engagement.
- Never present speculation as fact.
- Always distinguish evidence from belief.
- Cite primary sources (scripture, āgama, śāstra) before secondary sources.

### 2. Intellectual Honesty

- When evidence is uncertain, say so.
- When traditions disagree, present the different viewpoints.
- When scholarship debates a subject, explain the debate fairly.
- The archive educates; it does not persuade.

### 3. Respect for Tradition

- Respect all authentic sampradāyas and Guru-paramparā.
- Respect regional and oral traditions.
- Never ridicule sincerely held beliefs.
- Never sensationalize sacred subjects.

### 4. Preservation

- Many traditions survive only through temple customs, oral transmission, village elders, manuscripts, and maṭhas.
- The archive exists to preserve this knowledge responsibly before it disappears.

### 5. Curiosity

- Every piece of content should answer at least one question most people never thought to ask.
- The audience should finish every piece wanting to learn more.

### 6. Diversity Within Unity

- The archive does not treat Sanātana Dharma as a single homogeneous system.
- It recognizes multiple sampradāyas, darśanas, āgamas, tantric traditions, regional diversity, historical evolution, and living practices.
- Unity exists alongside diversity.

## Research Principles

| Principle | Meaning |
|-----------|---------|
| Tradition-first | Document what a tradition says before comparing it to others |
| Source hierarchy | Primary scripture → commentarial tradition → academic scholarship → popular sources |
| IAST standard | All Sanskrit transliteration uses IAST with proper diacritics (ā, ī, ū, ṛ, ṣ, ś, ṇ, ṭ, ḍ, ñ) |
| No anachronism | Present ideas in their historical context, not through modern frameworks |
| Falsifiability | Claims must be supported by textual or material evidence |

---

## Content Principles

| Principle | Meaning |
|-----------|---------|
| Platform-agnostic | Knowledge objects are created once and published across many platforms |
| Depth over breadth | A few deeply researched topics over many superficial ones |
| Visual storytelling | Design serves understanding, not decoration |
| Structured knowledge | Every piece connects to the larger knowledge graph |
| Reusability | Content should outlive any individual platform |

---

## Ethical Boundaries

- No content that denigrates other religions or traditions.
- No political commentary or partisan positioning.
- No content that could facilitate cultural appropriation or misrepresentation.
- No monetization that compromises editorial independence.
- No AI-generated claims presented without human verification.

---

## How These Principles Apply

These principles are not abstract ideals — they are decision-making tools:

- **Choosing topics:** Does this serve understanding or just engagement?
- **Writing content:** Is every claim backed by a cited source?
- **Visual design:** Does the design clarify or merely decorate?
- **Platform strategy:** Does this platform serve the knowledge, or does the knowledge serve the platform?
- **Collaboration:** Does this partner/contributor share our commitment to authenticity?
