# 1. Executive Summary

The Sanatana Dharma Knowledge Archive (SDKA) is a long-term digital preservation, research, and educational initiative dedicated to documenting, organizing, and presenting the authentic knowledge systems of Sanatana Dharma.

The archive is designed to become the foundation for multiple publishing platforms including:

- Instagram
- Website
- Digital Encyclopedia
- AI Knowledge Assistant
- Books
- Podcasts
- YouTube
- Educational Courses

Instagram is the first distribution platform—not the final product.

Every research note, illustration, carousel, article, and future publication should originate from a single, continuously evolving knowledge archive.

The archive must remain useful even if social media platforms disappear.

---

# 2. Vision

To build the world's most authentic, research-driven, visually engaging, and intellectually responsible public knowledge archive on Sanatana Dharma and the wider Indic Knowledge Systems.

The archive seeks to preserve not only scriptures but also:

- Temple traditions
- Regional practices
- Guru-parampara knowledge (where publicly discussable)
- Sacred geography
- Iconography
- Temple architecture
- Ritual systems
- Oral traditions
- Ancient literature
- Manuscript heritage
- Living traditions

The archive exists to preserve knowledge before it is forgotten.

---

# 3. Mission

The mission of SDKA is to:

- Research authentic sources.
- Organize scattered knowledge.
- Connect related traditions.
- Preserve regional heritage.
- Explain difficult concepts clearly.
- Inspire further study.
- Present visually compelling educational content.

The archive should never become a mythology page.

# 26. Practice Classification

Practices should always be classified by accessibility.

### Universal Devotional Practice

Examples:

- Temple darshan
- Public stotras
- Bhajans
- Nama japa (public traditions)

---

### Traditional Vrata

Common observances transmitted through families or temples.

---

### Public Spiritual Practice

Practices commonly performed without formal initiation in many traditions.

---

### Guru-Guided Practice

Traditionally learned under qualified guidance.

General information may be shared.

Specific methods should not.

---

### Initiation-Dependent Practice

Requires authentic initiation.

Never disclose:

- Restricted mantras
- Nyasa
- Yantras
- Mudras
- Ritual procedures
- Esoteric practices

Always recommend authentic Guru-parampara.
