# Purpose

This document defines how SDKA conducts research: the lifecycle every topic follows, source hierarchy, verification standards, and ethical boundaries.

For step-by-step daily workflow, see `03_Research_Manual_v2.md`.

---

# Research Philosophy

SDKA research answers:

> **"What is true according to this tradition?"**

—not—

> **"Which tradition is correct?"**

Document traditions before comparing them. Comparison should educate, not judge.

---

# Research Lifecycle

Every topic follows this lifecycle without skipping stages:

```
Discover
  ↓
Collect Sources
  ↓
Classify Sources
  ↓
Study Original Texts
  ↓
Study Traditional Commentaries
  ↓
Study Historical Evidence
  ↓
Compare Regional Traditions
  ↓
Identify Multiple Viewpoints
  ↓
Research Verification
  ↓
Knowledge Graph Integration
  ↓
Editorial Planning
  ↓
Visual Planning
  ↓
Content Production
  ↓
Publication
  ↓
Analytics
  ↓
Future Improvements
```

# Stage Definitions

## 1. Discover

**Trigger:** Backlog entry, graph gap, audience question, seasonal opportunity, field observation.

**Output:** Research question(s) written in a research card (`templates/research_card_template.md`).

**Questions to ask:**
- What do the earliest sources say?
- Why is this topic rarely discussed?
- Which tradition preserves this?
- What misconceptions exist?

---

## 2. Collect Sources

Gather all potentially relevant sources before reading deeply.

**Source types to search:**
- Primary scriptures and Agamas
- Puranas, stotras, commentaries
- Temple records and inscriptions
- Academic critical editions
- Regional oral accounts (with caution)
- Archaeological reports
- Manuscript catalogs

**Never begin with:** social media, Wikipedia alone, unverified blogs.

---

## 3. Classify Sources

Assign each source to a tier (see Source Hierarchy below). Record in the research card bibliography.

---

## 4–7. Study & Compare

Read original texts first, then commentaries, then historical evidence, then regional variations.

**During study, record:**
- Direct quotations with precise references (text, chapter, verse)
- Traditional commentator positions
- Regional differences
- Points of scholarly debate
- Gaps in evidence

---

## 8. Identify Multiple Viewpoints

When sampradayas disagree, document each:

| Sampradaya | Region | Source | Position |
|------------|--------|--------|----------|
| Example | Example | Example | Example |

Never declare one tradition "wrong."

---

## 9. Research Verification

Apply the **Myth Verification Framework** before any claim enters a knowledge object:

1. What is the earliest known source?
2. Is the claim scriptural? Agamic? Tantric?
3. Is it historical? Regional? Folklore?
4. When did the idea first appear?
5. Is there archaeological support?
6. What do traditional scholars say?
7. What do historians say?

Only then assign evidence tags and confidence scores.

---

## 10. Knowledge Graph Integration

Create or update the knowledge object. Add all graph connections. See `03_Knowledge_Object_Specification.md`.

---

## 11–14. Editorial, Visual, Production, Publication

Hand off to Editorial and Visual engines. Researcher's job is complete when the KO reaches `review` status.

---

## 15–16. Analytics & Future Improvements

After publication, monitor audience questions and new scholarship. Trigger KO updates or new backlog entries as needed.

# Source Hierarchy

| Tier | Authority | Examples |
|------|-----------|----------|
| **1 — Primary** | Highest | Vedas, Upanishads, Mahabharata, Valmiki Ramayana, Bhagavad Gita, Agamas, authentic Tantras, Dharma Shastras |
| **2 — Traditional** | High | Puranas, stotras, sahasranamas, traditional commentaries |
| **3 — Living** | Contextual | Temple rituals, Guru-parampara (public knowledge), regional customs, oral traditions |
| **4 — Historical** | Supplementary | Inscriptions, copper plates, archaeology, temple records, royal grants |
| **5 — Academic** | Supplementary | Critical editions, peer-reviewed journals, Indological studies |

**Rule:** Academic research supplements—it does not replace—traditional sources.

When tiers conflict, present both positions with evidence levels. Do not silently prefer one.

---

# Research Priorities

When multiple sources are available, consult in this order:

1. Original sources (Tier 1)
2. Traditional commentaries (Tier 2)
3. Temple traditions (Tier 3)
4. Historical evidence (Tier 4)
5. Regional traditions (Tier 3–4)
6. Modern academic research (Tier 5)

---

# Evidence Classification

Every published statement must be classifiable:

| Class | Definition |
|-------|------------|
| Scriptural | Explicitly in primary scripture |
| Agamic | In Agama literature |
| Tantric | In authentic Tantric texts or accepted lineage |
| Traditional | Guru-parampara or temple preservation |
| Historical | Inscriptions, archaeology, records |
| Regional | Specific to a region |
| Oral | Oral transmission primary |
| Folklore | Popular without strong textual support |
| Academic | Peer-reviewed scholarship |
| Speculative | Inference—not established |

---

# Confidence Matrix

Rate each major claim on three dimensions (1–5 stars):

- **Scriptural confidence**
- **Historical confidence**
- **Living tradition confidence**

Document the rating rationale in the knowledge object.

---

# Practice Classification

When researching practices, classify accessibility:

| Class | SDKA Treatment |
|-------|----------------|
| Universal devotional | Full public documentation |
| Traditional vrata | Document with context |
| Public spiritual | Document generally |
| Guru-guided | Context only; recommend Guru |
| Initiation-dependent | Never disclose methods; acknowledge existence only |

---

# Research Ethics

- Never sensationalize sacred knowledge.
- Never encourage experimentation with restricted practices.
- Never fabricate certainty.
- Never omit uncertainty.
- Never disrespect living traditions.
- Never reveal initiation-dependent knowledge.
- Always acknowledge differing traditions.
- Always separate scholarship from belief.
- Always preserve context.

---

# Continuous Discovery

SDKA does not rely on fixed topic lists. Continuously discover:

- Forgotten temples, saints, texts, rituals, symbolism
- Forgotten festivals, kingdoms, mathematics, astronomy, architecture
- Manuscripts remaining untranslated
- Regional traditions at risk of disappearance

Every discovery enters `Research_Backlog.md` immediately.

---

# Research Card

Use `templates/research_card_template.md` at the start of every research project. A research card tracks:

- Research questions
- Source bibliography with tiers
- Preliminary findings
- Evidence tags and confidence drafts
- Graph connection ideas
- Status and assignee

---

# Quality Checklist

Before marking a knowledge object `review`:

- [ ] At least 3 sources from Tier 1–3 consulted
- [ ] All claims have evidence tags
- [ ] Confidence scores assigned with rationale
- [ ] Multiple viewpoints documented (if applicable)
- [ ] Misconceptions section completed
- [ ] Graph connections defined (minimum met)
- [ ] No restricted content included
- [ ] Sources bibliography complete with references
- [ ] Open questions documented honestly
