# Purpose

This document defines how evidence is evaluated, classified, and cited within the Sanatana Dharma Knowledge Archive (SDKA). Its purpose is to ensure that every Knowledge Object applies a consistent standard of research quality while acknowledging the diversity of Hindu traditions.

---

# Core Principles

1. Distinguish evidence from interpretation.
2. Document uncertainty rather than hide it.
3. Respect regional and lineage diversity.
4. Never elevate popular claims to primary evidence.
5. Update classifications as stronger evidence becomes available.

---

# Source Hierarchy

| Rank | Source Category | Typical Examples | Default Weight |
|------|-----------------|------------------|----------------|
| A | Primary Sources | Vedas, Agamas, Tantras, authenticated scriptures, inscriptions | Highest |
| B | Scholarly Sources | Peer-reviewed research, critical editions, academic books | High |
| C | Living Traditions | Temple practice, priest guidance, oral continuity | Medium |
| D | Supporting Sources | Museum notes, heritage surveys, ASI reports | Medium |
| E | Discovery Sources | Websites, blogs, social media, videos | Discovery only |

---

# Evidence Levels

## Level A — Primary Evidence

Requirements:

- Direct textual support or verified architectural evidence.
- Source identified and documented.
- Preferred basis for factual claims.

Example:
A verified Agamic instruction regarding temple circulation.

---

## Level B — Strong Supporting Evidence

Requirements:

- Supported by reputable scholarly work.
- May interpret primary sources.
- Used when primary material is unavailable or incomplete.

---

## Level C — Living Tradition

Requirements:

- Observed temple practice.
- Oral explanations from recognized custodians.
- Regional customs preserved over time.

Must be labelled as living tradition rather than universal doctrine.

---

## Level D — Unverified or Disputed

Requirements:

- Frequently repeated but insufficiently evidenced.
- Contradictory traditions.
- Claims awaiting validation.

Such claims must never be presented as established fact.

---

# Confidence Ratings

| Rating | Meaning |
|---------|---------|
| High | Supported by multiple reliable sources with little contradiction. |
| Medium | Reasonable evidence exists but further validation is desirable. |
| Low | Limited evidence or conflicting interpretations. |
| Unknown | Evidence has not yet been evaluated. |

---

# Handling Conflicting Traditions

When traditions differ:

- Document each tradition separately.
- Identify the source tradition where possible.
- Avoid declaring one tradition universally correct.
- Record unresolved questions for future research.

---

# Citation Policy

Each major statement should include, where practical:

- Source category
- Evidence level
- Confidence rating
- Verification status

Direct quotations should identify the edition used.

---

# Regional Variation

Regional diversity is expected within Sanatana Dharma.

Researchers should:

- Record variation explicitly.
- Avoid replacing diversity with oversimplified summaries.
- Link variations to geography, temple lineage, or textual tradition where known.

---

# Evidence Review Workflow

1. Collect sources.
2. Classify each source.
3. Compare conflicting evidence.
4. Assign evidence level.
5. Assign confidence.
6. Record unresolved issues.
7. Update the Knowledge Object and Evidence Matrix.

---

# Minimum Repository Standard

Before a Knowledge Object reaches **R3 – Repository Ready**, it should include:

- At least one verified primary source (or documented absence).
- Supporting secondary literature where available.
- Completed Evidence Matrix.
- Confidence ratings for major claims.
- Bibliography.
- Misconception audit.
